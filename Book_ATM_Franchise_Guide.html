Chapter 8: Accountability


When you buy a franchise of an ATM, you are the owner of the franchise. But the business model I am referring to here gives you an additional benefit of owning an ATM franchise but not buying the ATM or other equipment. Sounds good, right?

What you need is to check with the ATM Operator that if they are selling the ATM to you, or just making you accountable for the location. If they ask you to buy the ATM, you must think about what will happen after the expiry of the franchise agreement. Will the ATM operator buy back the machine?

The best model I recommend is not to buy ATM or any other assets. Let the ATM Operator own those assets, and you being a franchisee focus on the profitability of the location. If you buy the ATM, then you will be responsible for its insurance and its depreciation too. Obviously, after a few years, what will you do with that old machine, it is not a refrigerator or a computer that after the expiry of the franchise agreement you will use for a different purpose or keep it with you.

Before signing an agreement with the franchisor / ATM Operator, you must ask the below questions: -

1. Who is the owner of the equipment?
2. Who is responsible for any physical damage or theft of the equipment?
3. Who is accountable for Cash in ATM?
